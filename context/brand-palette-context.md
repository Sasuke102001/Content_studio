# Brand Palette Context — @subrojitroy

**The visual identity should feel complete and dimensional, not limited to a single color.**

---

## Core Palette

### Primary Colors
- **Dark Gold / Amber:** #E6D3A3 — Authority, warmth, premium feel
- **Deep Charcoal / Black:** #0A0A0A, #121212 — Foundation, structure, minimal
- **Off-White / Neutral:** #F5F5F5, #D0D0D0 — Readability, contrast
- **Purple / Violet:** #7C3AED — Depth, intelligence, forward-looking energy

### Why This Combination
- Dark backgrounds provide focus and contrast
- Gold gives authority and premium feel
- Purple adds depth and emotional complexity
- Off-white ensures readability and breathing room

---

## Purple/Violet's Role

Purple should NOT overpower the brand. Instead, it should exist as:
- **Accent layer** — Highlights, emphasis, key moments
- **Subtle gradients** — Blending with gold or dark backgrounds
- **Supporting visual balance** — Creating dimension without competing for attention
- **Intelligence signal** — Suggesting depth and thoughtfulness beyond surface level

---

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Use Gold (#E6D3A3)
- Primary accent for most content
- Progress bars
- Key CTAs
- Main visual emphasis
- Avatar gradient

### When to Use Purple (#7C3AED)
- Secondary accent for depth
- Gradient combinations (especially with gold)
- Highlighting final insights or implications
- Creating visual variety across multiple posts
- Subtle background tints
- Key structural elements

### When to Use Both Together
- Avatar gradients (gold → purple)
- Conclusion slides or final statements
- Creating visual hierarchy across carousel sequences
- Emphasizing the "system" or "framework" elements

---

## Visual Principles

The palette should feel:
- **Premium** — Intentional color choices, not arbitrary
- **Minimal** — No color overload, every choice serves purpose
- **Structured** — Colors reinforce information hierarchy
- **Intelligent** — Suggests depth and careful thinking
- **Slightly editorial** — Professional but not corporate
- **Not flat** — Gradients and layering add dimension
- **Not monochrome** — Purple prevents it from being one-note

---

## System vs. Single Color

❌ **Wrong:** Brand built only around gold tones (flat, one-dimensional, warm but limited)

✓ **Right:** System where gold and purple work together (dimensional, intelligent, premium, complete)

---

## In Practice

**Across carousel posts:**
- Most slides use gold for accents
- Final/conclusion slides can emphasize purple for depth
- Gradients combine both for richness
- Alternating light/dark backgrounds create structure
- Off-white text ensures readability

**In avatars and brand marks:**
- Gold → Purple gradient shows both colors working together
- Creates visual sophistication and forward-thinking feel

**In CTAs and key moments:**
- Gold remains primary (authority)
- Purple can support (depth) in final asks or conclusions

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **Don't make purple primary** — It's an accent layer, not the foundation
2. **Don't eliminate gold** — Gold is the authority and warmth
3. **Don't use purple alone** — Always in relation to gold or as subtle accent
4. **Don't make it flat** — Gradients and layering create dimension
5. **Don't over-explain the palette** — Let it work visually, not conceptually

---

## Why This Matters

The palette is a **system**, not a color choice. Together, gold and purple signal:
- Maturity (not startup hype)
- Intelligence (not just style)
- Completeness (not one-dimensional)
- Premium positioning (not mass market)
- Forward-thinking (not stuck in one aesthetic)
