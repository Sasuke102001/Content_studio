## Writing Style Preferences

### Sentence Structure
- Short and clear when precision matters
- Longer when explaining structure, causation, or systems
- No filler, no bridge sentences
- No "as mentioned earlier" or "to clarify" padding

### Argument Structure
- Lead with the insight, not the setup
- Show the pattern, not the journey
- Give the mechanism, not the story
- "Here's what I noticed" (not "I spent months figuring out")
- "The gap is X because Y happens" (not "there's a gap and here's my feel")
- "This means Z" (not "wouldn't it be cool if")

### Paragraph Structure
- One idea per paragraph
- Separate the observation from the implication
- Use whitespace to let ideas breathe
- Short punchy paragraphs for emphasis, longer ones for complex ideas

### What to Avoid
- Corporate language ("leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem," "at scale")
- Generic founder narrative ("the journey," "I had a realization," "we're disrupting")
- Inspirational fluff (no "dream big," no vague calls to action, no "together we can")
- Over-polished brand speak
- Vagueness or hand-waving
- Flattening me into a single identity
- Making me sound like something I'm not
- Emotional manipulation or fake intimacy
- Building fake authority ("I've cracked the code," "here's the secret")

### Tone in Practice
- Confident but not arrogant
- Precise but not pedantic
- Direct but not rude
- Philosophical but grounded
- Pattern-focused but not dismissive of execution
