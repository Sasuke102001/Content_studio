# Carousel Content Context — @subrojitroy

**How to apply Roy's personal voice to carousel posts on Instagram/LinkedIn.**

This file is separate from roys-personal-voice.md and focuses specifically on carousel structure, messaging, and tone.

---

## Carousel Philosophy

A carousel is a **structured argument in 5 slides**, not a story.

Each slide should:
- Make ONE point clearly
- Connect logically to the next slide
- Build toward a position or insight
- Reward someone reading all the way through

**NOT:**
- Pretty decoration around a vague idea
- Emotional journey ("here's my story")
- Listicle format
- Generic founder advice

---

## Carousel Structure for Roy's Voice

### Slide 1: The Observation (Hook)
- Lead with something specific you noticed
- Not a question, not a teaser
- State it directly
- Example: "Venues spend ₹10K–15K per week on entertainment and measure nothing."
- Tone: Matter-of-fact, specific

### Slide 2: Why This Matters (Gap/Problem)
- Explain the consequence or inefficiency
- Show the cost of not knowing
- NOT emotional ("this is sad"), NOT accusatory ("venues are dumb")
- Structural: "When X happens and you don't measure Y, this occurs"
- Example: "They're making ₹100L+ decisions on feel instead of data."
- Tone: Clear-eyed, precise

### Slide 3: The Mechanism (How/Why)
- Show how the pattern works
- Explain the underlying structure
- This is where Roy brings systems thinking
- Example: "Revenue depends on: baseline behavior + intervention + measurement. Skip measurement and you lose the feedback loop."
- Tone: Structured, analytical

### Slide 4: The Solution/Framework (What I Built)
- Show the system or approach
- Be specific about what makes it different
- NOT "we're the only ones" or "we're disrupting"
- Structural: "I built a system that..."
- Example: "Baseline → Profile → Strategy → Execute → Measure → Pattern → Decision → Scale"
- Tone: Operator confidence, not hype

### Slide 5: The Implication (Why This Matters Broadly)
- Connect to the bigger picture
- Hint at scalability beyond the current domain
- NOT "this will change the world"
- Structural: "When venues have this data, X becomes possible"
- Example: "This system works for any domain where measurement is weak."
- Tone: Philosophical but grounded

## Messaging Strategy by Carousel Type

### Type 1: Problem + System Carousel (Founder Positioning)

**Purpose:** Establish Roy as a systems builder, not domain expert

**Structure:**
1. Observation about unmeasured lever in [domain]
2. Why it matters (business consequence)
3. How the lever actually works (mechanism)
4. System I built to measure it
5. Implication: This scales beyond [domain]

**Tone:**
- "Here's what I noticed"
- "The gap isn't about [domain], it's about measurement"
- "I built this because weak measurement bothers me"
- Focus on framework, not domain expertise

**Avoid:**
- "I love [domain]"
- "I'm passionate about [domain]"
- "The [domain] industry needs this"
- Making the domain central to identity

### Type 2: Data/Contribution Carousel (Form Promotion)

**Purpose:** Ask for data contribution without sounding like you're asking for a favor

**Structure:**
1. Gap in current data/knowledge
2. What data exists (and what doesn't)
3. How measurement works in practice
4. Ask for contribution (frame it as "you're building the dataset")
5. Outcome (what becomes possible when data exists)

**Tone:**
- "This data is invisible to venues"
- "You have the information they need"
- "Contribution" frame, not "help us" frame
- Specific ask (form link, 2 minutes, honest answers)

**Avoid:**
- "Help us out"
- "Please fill out the form"
- "We need your data"
- Making it about what venues need (make it about what data reveals)

## Slide Copy Guidelines

### Headline (Slide h1)
- **Length:** 6-10 words max for dark/light backgrounds, 8-12 for carousel slides
- **Style:** No questions (unless rhetorical), no soft language
- **Voice:** Direct statement or observation
- **Examples:**
  - ✓ "Venues spend ₹10K–15K per show. Measure nothing."
  - ✓ "The data is invisible"
  - ✓ "Entertainment becomes a system, not a guess"
  - ✗ "What if venues could measure entertainment?"
  - ✗ "Unlocking the power of data"

### Body Text (Slide p)
- **Length:** 15-30 words per paragraph for mobile readability
- **Style:** Short sentences, one idea per line
- **Voice:** Specific, concrete, measurable
- **Examples:**
  - ✓ "They don't track customer acquisition cost per show. They don't track revenue change during music. They're guessing."
  - ✓ "Revenue is determined by: baseline + intervention + measurement"
  - ✗ "Venues should think about how to leverage data more strategically"
  - ✗ "We're so excited to share this journey with you"

### Emphasis & Accent
- Use accent color only for key terms or final insight
- Not for motivational language or hype
- Highlights should be structural (steps, numbers, key concepts)

## Tone Calibration Across Slides

| Slide | Tone | Feel | Energy |
|-------|------|------|--------|
| 1: Observation | Matter-of-fact | Direct, specific | Grounded |
| 2: Gap | Analytical | Clear-eyed, precise | Measured |
| 3: Mechanism | Academic | Structured, system-focused | Intellectual |
| 4: Solution | Operator | Confident, practical | Action-oriented |
| 5: Implication | Philosophical | Scaling, pattern-oriented | Visionary (subtle) |

**Overall:** Professional + accessible. Not corporate, not casual. Think operator thinking in public.

## What NOT to Do in Roy's Carousels

❌ Emotional storytelling ("here's my journey")  
❌ Generic founder advice ("7 lessons I learned")  
❌ Before/after narrative ("we were struggling, then we...")  
❌ Hype language ("game-changing," "revolutionizing," "disrupting")  
❌ Fake intimacy ("join us on this adventure")  
❌ Domain expertise positioning ("I'm an [industry] expert")  
❌ Motivation or inspiration ("you've got this," "believe in yourself")  
❌ Personal anecdotes (stories about Roy's experiences)  
❌ Building authority through false scarcity ("only X people know this")  
❌ Algorithmic optimization (write for clicks, not clarity)

## Polynovea Carousel Specific Rules

When carousel is about Polynovea:

1. **Lead with measurement/systems, not venues**
   - ✓ "Venues have an unmeasured lever"
   - ✗ "Entertainment is powerful"

2. **Position venues as testbed, not identity**
   - ✓ "First proving it at venues. Next: [thinking bigger]"
   - ✗ "We're building for the live music industry"

3. **Show the framework, not the domain story**
   - ✓ "Baseline → Profile → Strategy → Execute → Measure → Pattern → Decision → Scale"
   - ✗ "We fell in love with live music"

4. **Make the data/measurement central**
   - ✓ "This system works for any domain where measurement is weak"
   - ✗ "We're passionate about venues"

5. **Operator voice, not founder narrative**
   - ✓ "I noticed venues don't measure X, built a system"
   - ✗ "We had a vision to transform entertainment"

## Form Promotion Carousel Specific Rules

When carousel is asking for data/form response:

1. **Lead with the gap in knowledge, not the ask**
   - ✓ Slide 1: "This data is invisible"
   - ✗ Slide 1: "Fill out our form"

2. **Make it about what data reveals, not what venues need**
   - ✓ "When enough venues have this data, entertainment stops being a guess"
   - ✗ "Help venues make better decisions"

3. **Frame contribution, not request**
   - ✓ "You're contributing to the dataset that fixes how venues optimize"
   - ✗ "We need your help"

4. **Specific ask, minimal friction**
   - ✓ "2 minutes. Honest answers. Link in bio."
   - ✗ "If you have a few minutes, please consider helping"

5. **Outcome should be about systems/data, not gratitude**
   - ✓ "Pattern becomes a system. System scales beyond venues."
   - ✗ "Thanks for being part of our journey"

## Formatting Standards

### Typography
- Headline font: Bold, clear, high contrast
- Body text: Regular weight, 14-16px minimum
- Spacing: Generous padding, whitespace matters
- Line height: 1.6+ for readability

### Colors
- Dark background (#0A0A0A or #121212) for contrast
- Light text (#F5F5F5 or #D0D0D0)
- Accent color (#E6D3A3 gold or #7C3AED violet) only for emphasis
- No gradients except on CTA or final slide

### Layout
- Content centered
- Progress bar at bottom (shows position in carousel)
- Dot indicators for slide count
- 420px fixed width (Instagram standard)
- 4:5 aspect ratio per slide

### Mobile Readiness
- All text readable at phone size
- No small text, no dense paragraphs
- Single idea per slide
- Whitespace for visual rhythm

## Quality Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] Does each slide make ONE point clearly?
- [ ] Does the headline sound like Roy thinking out loud (not brand copy)?
- [ ] Is the mechanism shown, not just the outcome?
- [ ] Are specific numbers/facts used, not vague claims?
- [ ] Is there zero hype language ("revolutionary," "game-changing")?
- [ ] Does it bring academic rigor to a practical problem?
- [ ] Could someone not familiar with Polynovea follow the logic?
- [ ] Is the final slide about implication, not CTA alone?
- [ ] Does it feel like systems thinking, not domain expertise?
- [ ] Would Roy actually say this out loud to an intelligent peer?

## Carousel Sequencing Strategy

Roy's account carousels should build a narrative arc across multiple posts:

**Wave 1: Founder Positioning**
- Carousel 1: "How I think about unmeasured levers" (systems founder intro)
- Carousel 2: "The data gap at venues" (specific problem)

**Wave 2: Data Ask**
- Carousel 3: "Contributing to the dataset" (form promotion, measurement focus)

**Wave 3: Deeper Thinking**
- Carousel 4: "Why measurement enables optimization" (philosophical)
- Carousel 5: "Scaling measurement frameworks" (beyond venues)

**Wave 4: Proof**
- Carousel 6: "What the data shows" (results/patterns)

**Ongoing:** Mix in perspectives on systems, markets, behavior that aren't Polynovea-specific.
